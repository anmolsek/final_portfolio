work about

Kin

connecting seniors through social media

team

two ui/ux designers

timeline

sept 2023 - dec 2023

4 months

tools

figma, protopie

my role

user research, design system, wireframing, mockups, prototyping

project overview

This 4 month project involved using the user group of seniors which are 60 years and older and creating a digital app as a solution to one of their problems. Our team decided that we would focus on the concept of communication and connection which influenced our design decisions and the trajectory of the project.

my impact

I was involved in making key decisions for the project such as what direction we want to go in, specific style choices, the user experience for the group, conducting user interviews, creating mockups, and designing wireframes using Figma.

final product

ux research + problem space

Using the target group, I conducted research to identify any problems and simply look at data to guide the project. After some analysis, I was presented with this statistic that ultimately drove our project to a social media platform.

“[A] report indicated that almost 1 out of 4 community dwelling older Americans, adults aged over 65 living outside of nursing homes and assisted living facilities, are socially isolated, and almost half of those over age 60 reported feeling lonely.”

-Strengthening Social Connections in Older Adults

personas

Now that our focus was on socially isolated seniors and helping them connect, I helped create personas that were around our target group so that we could see from their perspective on what their lives were like. This helped with narrowing the scope to specific problems

brainstorming designs

With the research conducted, our team of 3 designers each came up with an initial design that we thought best suited for the app. We agreed on a similar color palette and came up with the designs on our own. The design I created is the first one. We had chosen to go with a social media app because of the need for connection but also introduce some features like messaging, posting, and adding calendar events so that the senior was well integrated into their family's lives.

interactive prototype

Once we decided on a final design, we began to create some sort of prototype. Here, I made some of the wireframes for the prototype such as the calendar page. The prototype was then put on Protopie where then all the frames were linked together

usability testing

I conducted user interviews with 2 seniors that were over 60 years old and allowed them to explore the app. My interviews were done individually and I recorded notes as seen on the right on what their thoughts were, any frustrations, and any confusions. We were theoretically creating a solution however without usability testing there was no way of confirming this

putting this all together

Bringing the notes back to my team, we discussed some of the issues that were noted and began editing the wireframes once again. I edited wireframes and also helped create new elements like a new navigation bar on the bottom as the previous was too confusing with the use of the icons

conclusion

With the issues addressed, the final prototype was ready. Kin is a social media app with multiple features like calendar, adding events, messaging, and a personalized timeline. This prototype was created on Protopie again and shows all the different states of the app.

reflection

This project immensely helped my personal growth into app design, ui/ux design, ux research. I learned the importance of design choices and how every decision matters with a proper understanding behind it. Working in a team taught me the importance of collaborating on a singular vision we had and being able to take feedback and apply it. Finally, I learned how to address specific user needs and have the ability to empathize with them and step into their perspective to get a better understanding of the problem and what I can do as the solution.
